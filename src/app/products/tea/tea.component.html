Rice and Wheat

Tea, a beloved beverage enjoyed worldwide, is made from the dried leaves of the Camellia sinensis plant. With a rich history spanning centuries, tea has cultural significance in many societies, from ceremonial traditions to daily rituals. The main types of tea include black, green, white, oolong, and herbal, each offering unique flavors, aromas, and health benefits. Black tea, the most oxidized type, boasts robust flavor and is often enjoyed with milk and sugar. Green tea, minimally oxidized, is renowned for its fresh taste and potential health-promoting properties due to its high antioxidant content. White tea, made from young leaves and buds, has a delicate flavor profile and is prized for its subtle nuances. Oolong tea falls between green and black tea in oxidation, offering a wide range of flavors from floral to fruity. Herbal teas, not technically "tea" as they are not made from Camellia sinensis, encompass a variety of infusions made from herbs, spices, and botanicals. Popular herbal teas include chamomile, peppermint, and rooibos, each with its own unique flavor and potential health benefits. Tea culture extends beyond the beverage itself, encompassing social rituals, relaxation, and even wellness practices. Whether sipped hot or cold, tea remains a cherished beverage enjoyed by people around the globe.
